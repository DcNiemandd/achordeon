# Songbooks

To filter out songs you want to play, you can make custom songbooks. The songbook is just an ordered list of songs. There is always one default songbook named **All songs**; its order is read-only and songs cannot be removed from it.

The same song can appear multiple times in a songbook.

<Image alt="Showcase songbook view" isDesign />

In the left panel you can see list of your songs. The list is the same as in `Songs` module but has some functionality disabled. On the right is your songbook list.

You can put songs in the songbook. With buttons you can insert selected songs to the start, to the end, above selected songbook song, or below. Second way is to drag&drop - dragging selected songs by the handle will show you, where will you be dropping them in the songbook.

You can reorder the songs. You can use buttons on the selected songs to move the song one song over, to the start or to the end. You can also use handle to drag&drop the song.

:::note[Remove vs delete]
Removing a song from a songbook won't delete the song. The song can be deleted only in the `Songs` module.
:::

A songbook can restyle the chords of all its songs - chord color, chord size and font are songbook-scoped settings.

Each songbook in the list carries its own actions on the row: rename, duplicate, download (as a PDF) and export (to a file), and delete. Duplicating one makes an independent copy with the same songs — the songs are only referenced, so nothing is copied twice.

The **All songs** book can be downloaded (a PDF of your whole library) and exported, but not renamed, duplicated, deleted or opened — it is the library itself, not a book you made. To browse your songs, use the `Songs` module.

Because All songs has no order of its own, its download dialog lets you choose one: by song title, library name, or date (created or changed), in either direction, with an option to put your favorites first. A real songbook keeps the order you arranged, so it has no such choice. Your last choice is remembered.

## Download

When downloading the songbook, you can set some options, that will affect the exported PDF.

- **Title page**: title, subtitle, author (the songbook's own fields, separate from any song's title and subtitle). It is a rendered page of the songbook, so it wears the book's own chord colours and fonts — the same page you see previewed in `Songbooks`, centred on the sheet.
- **Summary**: a contents list of every song against its page number. Each line is a link — tap it to jump to the song. The title page and the summary are not numbered; the first song is page 1.
- **Print**: paper size (A4, Letter or A5), portrait or landscape, the page margin, and where the page numbers sit (any corner or centred, top or bottom)

Your last print choices are remembered, so the next download opens on the same paper.

:::note[Print]
Printing settings will not change the aspect ratio of a song — every song keeps its own shape and is scaled to fit the page, so a song shaped unlike the paper sits centred with white either side.
:::
